Governing Physics

Gyroscopic Precession

A spinning disc acts as a gyroscope. When an external moment is applied (from aerodynamic pitching), the disc doesn't simply pitch—it precesses about the spin axis, converting pitch moment into roll rate.

Roll Rate from Precession
φ̇ = −M / (ω × ΔI)

Where M is the pitching moment, ω is spin rate, and ΔI = Ixy − Iz is the moment of inertia difference. For a disc, ΔI < 0, which determines the precession direction.

Asymmetric Edge Vortices

The spinning disc creates asymmetric flow conditions at its edges. The advancing edge (moving with the flow) generates a stronger tip vortex than the retreating edge.

Key Insight

This vortex asymmetry produces a net rolling moment that, combined with gyroscopic coupling, causes the characteristic S-curve flight path: high-speed turn followed by low-speed fade.

The visualizations show these vortices—larger spirals indicate stronger circulation on the advancing edge.
